Research

JMP

Job-market paper · with Jiri Havel · preliminary draft available on request

Inventories, Trade Credit, and Imported Inputs in Firm Dynamics

Using confidential Belgian monthly customs transactions by firm, HS6 product, and origin/destination country linked to firm balance sheets, we study how importers finance trade. Preliminary evidence shows that bank credit and trade credit are substitutes for financially constrained firms, and that importers rely more on bank credit relative to trade credit when suppliers are farther away.

We develop a dynamic importer model in which trade credit is an intertemporal financing choice for imported-input purchases.

Expected full draft: Fall 2026 · Draft available on request

02

Working paper · with Kazuma Inagaki · presented at ETSG 2025

From Fewer Workers to Fewer Exporters? Population Growth and Exporter Dynamics

Declining labor-force growth reduces firm entry, shifts activity toward larger incumbents, and raises exporter participation. In the calibrated model, a one percentage point decline in labor-force growth raises the exporter share by about 1.1 percentage points, but explains only a small share of the observed rise in U.S. exporter participation.

Draft available on request

03

Work in progress · solo-authored project

Disclosed Supply-Chain Risk, Inventory Adjustment, and the Trade-Credit Channel

I build supply-chain, trade-credit, and working-capital risk measures from 136,056 SEC filings for U.S. public firms, 2018–2024. Supply-chain risk language predicts future inventory accumulation, especially raw-material buffers, while trade-credit language is more informative about contemporaneous receivables, payables, and industry-level payables adjustment.

Preliminary draft expected Spring 2027
