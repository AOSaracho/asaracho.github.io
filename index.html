Research

JMP

Job-market paper · with Jiri Havel

Inventories, Trade Credit, and Imported Inputs in Firm Dynamics

We study how trade credit, inventory buffers, and imported-input use interact in firm dynamics. Using confidential Belgian customs and balance-sheet data, we document the links between import exposure, inventories, accounts payable, supplier finance, and firm outcomes.

A dynamic model quantifies how the availability and cost of supplier finance shape inventory policy, firm growth, and exit, particularly in trade-intensive sectors.

Preliminary draft available on request · Full draft expected Fall 2026

02

Working paper · with Kazuma Inagaki

From Fewer Workers to Fewer Exporters? Population Growth and Exporter Dynamics

A dynamic heterogeneous-firm model and U.S. state-level evidence show that slower labor-force growth reduces entry and shifts the firm distribution toward larger incumbents. The mechanism raises exporter participation, but explains only a small share of its long-run U.S. increase.

Presented at ETSG 2025 · Draft available on request

03

Work in progress · solo-authored

Disclosed Supply-Chain Risk, Inventory Adjustment, and the Trade-Credit Channel

Using 136,056 SEC filings from 2018–2024, I construct measures of supply-chain, trade-credit, and working-capital risk and test whether disclosure language predicts inventories, receivables, payables, and subsequent firm adjustment.

Preliminary draft expected Spring 2027
