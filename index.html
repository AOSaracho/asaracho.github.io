Research

JMP

Job-market paper · with Jiri Havel

Inventories, Trade Credit, and Imported Inputs in Firm Dynamics

We study how firms finance imported-input purchases when supplier distance, inventory needs, and financial constraints shape the choice between bank credit and trade credit. Using confidential Belgian monthly customs transactions linked to firm balance sheets, we find that bank credit and trade credit are substitutes for financially constrained firms.

The evidence shows that importers rely more on bank credit relative to trade credit when suppliers are farther away. We develop a dynamic importer model in which trade credit is an intertemporal financing choice for imported-input purchases.

Preliminary draft available on request · Expected full draft Fall 2026

02

Working paper · with Kazuma Inagaki

From Fewer Workers to Fewer Exporters? Population Growth and Exporter Dynamics

A dynamic heterogeneous-firm model and U.S. state-level evidence show that slower labor-force growth reduces entry, shifts activity toward larger incumbents, and raises exporter participation. In the calibrated model, a one percentage point decline in labor-force growth raises the exporter share by about 1.1 percentage points, but explains only a small share of the long-run U.S. increase.

Presented at ETSG 2025 · Draft available on request

03

Work in progress · solo-authored

Disclosed Supply-Chain Risk, Inventory Adjustment, and the Trade-Credit Channel

Using 136,056 SEC filings from 2018–2024, I construct measures of supply-chain, trade-credit, and working-capital risk. Supply-chain risk language predicts future inventory accumulation, while industry-level trade-credit risk predicts firms’ payables adjustment.

Preliminary draft expected Spring 2027
